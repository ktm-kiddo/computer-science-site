Tests for Gases

Gas Test Positive result
Ammonia (NH₃) Hold damp red litmus paper near the gas Paper turns blue
Carbon dioxide (CO₂) Bubble gas through limewater Limewater turns cloudy white
Chlorine (Cl₂) Hold damp blue litmus paper near the gas Paper is bleached white (first turns red, then white)
Hydrogen (H₂) Hold a burning splint near the gas Squeaky pop
Oxygen (O₂) Hold a glowing splint near the gas Splint relights

Flame Tests

Ion Flame colour
Lithium (Li⁺) Red
Sodium (Na⁺) Yellow
Potassium (K⁺) Lilac
Calcium (Ca²⁺) Orange-red
Copper (Cu²⁺) Blue-green

Tests for Cations (add NaOH solution)

Ion Observation Precipitate formula
Iron(II) (Fe²⁺) Pale green precipitate Fe(OH)₂
Iron(III) (Fe³⁺) Orange/brown precipitate Fe(OH)₃
Copper(II) (Cu²⁺) Light blue precipitate Cu(OH)₂
Ammonium (NH₄⁺) Warm → ammonia gas released (turns damp red litmus blue) —

Tests for Anions

Ion Reagent(s) Positive result
Carbonate (CO₃²⁻) Add dilute acid; bubble through limewater Limewater turns cloudy white (CO₂ produced)
Chloride (Cl⁻) Acidify with dilute HNO₃, then add AgNO₃ White precipitate (AgCl)
Bromide (Br⁻) Acidify with dilute HNO₃, then add AgNO₃ Cream precipitate (AgBr)
Iodide (I⁻) Acidify with dilute HNO₃, then add AgNO₃ Yellow precipitate (AgI)
Sulfate (SO₄²⁻) Acidify with dilute HCl, then add BaCl₂ White precipitate (BaSO₄)

Test for Water

Type Method Positive result
Chemical (presence) Add anhydrous copper(II) sulfate (white) Turns blue (CuSO₄·5H₂O)
Physical (purity) Measure boiling point Exactly 100 °C
